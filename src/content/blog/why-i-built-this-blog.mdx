This is the first post on a blog I built from scratch, on my own domain, with
the same stack that powers the rest of this site: Next.js, Tailwind, and MDX.

I write elsewhere too. But there's a difference between renting space on a
platform and owning the thing itself. When the words live at `dkoike.com`, the
design, the URL, the reading experience — and whatever I learn building it —
all compound here, under my name.

## What this is for

Mostly: thinking out loud about privacy-first AI, the move from physics to
computer science, and the small engineering decisions that don't fit anywhere
else. Some posts will be technical write-ups. Some will be shorter notes.

## Why MDX

Because a post is just Markdown until it isn't. MDX lets me drop a real
component into the middle of an article — an interactive demo, a chart, a live
example — without leaving the page. That flexibility is the whole reason to host
writing yourself rather than paste it into a text box.

> The best portfolio piece for an engineer is often the thing the portfolio is
> built with.
